Lindy Hop and Jazz DJing

Lindy Hop

I started dancing lindy hop — a genre of swing dancing — in September 2013, and haven't stopped since. I regularly compete, travel for dancing, and teach as both a lead and a follow. Additionally, I have choreographed and performed with numerous groups — including the Sproul Stompers, the [M]ovement, and the East Baes. My joy in lindy hop is derived from connecting with my partner, and interpreting the music together through conversation in dance.

Jazz DJing

Shortly after learning to dance, I began to DJ jazz music within my college scene. I started casually, but as my love for jazz evolved I grew more serious. Three years later, I have become a regular DJ at many venues in the Bay Area and Seattle, and have taught a class series for aspiring new jazz DJs in Seattle. It is my belief that the job of a swing DJ is two-fold: (1) to play music that makes people stay as long as possible, and (2) to play music that encourages dancers' growth, whether they've been dancing for one day or one decade. My favorite bandleaders and musicians include Sidney Bechet, Chick Webb, Artie Shaw, Hot Lips Page, Ella Fitzgerald, and Earl Hines.
