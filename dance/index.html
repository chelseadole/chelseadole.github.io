Lindy Hop and Jazz DJing

Lindy Hop

I started dancing lindy hop — a genre of swing dancing — in September 2013, and haven't stopped since. I regularly compete, travel for dancing, and teach as both a lead and a follow. Additionally, I have choreographed and performed with numerous groups — including the Sproul Stompers, the [M]ovement, and the East Baes. My joy in lindy hop is derived from connecting with my partner, and interpreting the music together through conversation in dance.

Jazz DJing

Shortly after learning to dance, I began to DJ jazz music within the UC Berkeley college scene. I started casually, but as my love for jazz evolved I grew more serious. Three years later, I have become a regular DJ at many weekly venues in the Bay Area and Seattle, including Wednesday Night Hop, The Breakaway, Cat's Corner, The Century Ballroom, Revival Rhythm, Savoy Mondays, and Eastside Stomp. Additionally, I taught a class series for aspiring new jazz DJs in Seattle, played for several local lindy exchanges, and acted as Head DJ for Big Sky Weekend in 2018. My favorite swing-era bandleaders and musicians include Sidney Bechet, Chick Webb, Artie Shaw, Hot Lips Page, Ella Fitzgerald, and Earl Hines.
